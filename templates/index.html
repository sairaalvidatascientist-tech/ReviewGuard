Introduction

Online reviews strongly influence customer decisions on e-commerce platforms like Amazon, eBay, and Alibaba. However, many sellers manipulate ratings by posting fake or deceptive reviews. This leads to customers purchasing low-quality products, honest businesses losing customers, and decreased trust in online marketplaces. Our system uses NLP to analyze review text, sentiment patterns, and user behavior to automatically detect suspicious reviews.

Problem Statement

Manual detection of fake reviews is difficult due to the large volume of daily reviews, realistic-looking fake content, and multiple fake accounts. A machine learning system can detect patterns that humans may miss, providing a scalable and accurate solution.

Objectives

  • Automatically identify fake product reviews
  • Analyze text patterns and sentiment
  • Detect suspicious reviewer behavior
  • Provide predictions through a simple web interface

System Architecture & Methods

1. Data Collection

Utilizing datasets such as Amazon Review Dataset and Yelp Fake Review Dataset containing text, ratings, and labels.

2. Preprocessing

Cleaning text by removing punctuation, stop words, and applying tokenization/lemmatization via NLP libraries (NLTK, spaCy).

3. Detection Methods

  • Pattern Analysis: Repeated phrases & overly positive language.
  • Sentiment Analysis: Evaluating scores via TextBlob.
  • Account Detection: Abnormal reviewing behavior mapping.

Technology Stack

Python NLTK spaCy TextBlob scikit-learn Flask Matplotlib

Expected Results

75–90% accuracy in detecting fake reviews with real-time analysis capabilities.

Future Improvements & Applications

  • Deep learning models & bot detection (e.g., BERT)
  • Reviewer network analysis
  • Fake image detection

Applications: E-commerce platforms, product review websites, online marketplaces, and consumer protection agencies.
